1. The UPI PIN is for paying: Never enter your UPI PIN to receive money. The UPI PIN is only used to deduct money from your account when you are making a payment with the UPI PIN.

2. Verify the receiver: double-check the merchants name or the persons name on your screen before you authorize a UPI payment with the UPI PIN.

3. Avoid using Wi-Fi for banking: Never pay your semester fees or check your bank balances on unsecured campus Wi-Fi or cafe Wi-Fi when you are using the UPI PIN for a payment.

Account and Device Security

4. Enable the Two-Factor Authentication: Turn on the Two-Factor Authentication for your banking apps and your emails and your social media accounts to keep your UPI PIN and account safe.

5. Use passwords for everything: Never use the same password for your college portal and your bank account where you have the UPI PIN.

6. Update your phone and apps: Regularly update your phones operating system and your payment apps to fix security problems and keep your UPI PIN safe.

Social Media Habits

7. Do not click on "links: Ignore unverified SMS or emails that claim your fees are pending or offer you fake internships when you are using the UPI PIN.

8. Stop sharing much: Never post photos of your college ID card or your boarding passes or your real-time locations on social media where people can see your UPI PIN or account details.

9. Avoid things that are traps: Stay away from pirated textbooks or free OTT links because they are common traps, for malware that can steal your UPI PIN and account details.
